# ## Multi-Modal Integrated Analysis of Mitochondrial Dynamics and Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP) Modulation via Targeted Circular RNA Delivery for Enhanced Geroprotection

**Abstract:** This research proposes a novel framework for mitigating age-related decline by dynamically modulating mitochondrial function and reducing the detrimental effects of the Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP). Utilizing targeted circular RNA (circRNA) delivery, we aim to selectively regulate key mitochondrial biogenesis and quality control genes while simultaneously suppressing pro-inflammatory cytokine release characteristic of SASP. **1. Introduction:**

The biological senescence process is characterized by irreversible cell cycle arrest and the development of the SASP, a complex mixture of cytokines, chemokines, and extracellular vesicles which contribute to chronic inflammation, tissue dysfunction, and age-related disease progression.  Concurrently, age-related mitochondrial dysfunction, including reduced oxidative phosphorylation efficiency, increased reactive oxygen species (ROS) production, and impaired mitophagy, further accelerates senescence and amplifies the SASP. Therefore modulating both mitochondrial dynamics and SASP synergistically presents a highly attractive therapeutic strategy.  Current approaches often lack specificity and systemic control. Our research introduces a targeted circRNA delivery system coupled with advanced data analytics to address these shortcomings, providing a personalized and highly adaptable therapeutic intervention.

**2. Theoretical Foundations & Methodology:**

Our framework utilizes established concepts in circRNA biology, mitochondrial bioenergetics, and machine learning. CircRNAs are highly stable, non-coding RNA molecules that can act as miRNA sponges, regulating gene expression post-transcriptionally. We exploit this property by designing circRNAs targeting specific miRNA regulators of mitochondrial genes (PGC-1Œ±, NRF1, TFAM) to enhance mitochondrial biogenesis and quality control. Simultaneously, circRNAs targeting miRNAs regulating pro-inflammatory SASP factors (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-Œ±) will attenuate SASP production.

**2.2 Semantic & Structural Decomposition Module (Parser):**

A transformer-based Language Model, fine-tuned on a curated database of biological literature and experimental results, parses this high-dimensional data, identifying key relationships between mitochondrial dysfunction, SASP, and cellular senescence.  Graph neural networks are then employed to construct relational network models that capture the complex dependencies between genes, proteins, metabolites, and cellular phenotypes.

**2.3 Multi-layered Evaluation Pipeline:**

This pipeline incorporates multiple tiers of validation:

* **Logic Consistency Engine:** Automated theorem provers (Lean4) verify the logical consistency of hypotheses derived from the network model regarding circRNA targeting and its predicted impact on mitochondrial function and SASP.
* **Formula & Code Verification Sandbox:**  Simulations of metabolic flux, ROS production, and cytokine signaling pathways use validated mathematical models and executable code within a contained environment to permit extensive parameter sweeps and risk mitigation.
