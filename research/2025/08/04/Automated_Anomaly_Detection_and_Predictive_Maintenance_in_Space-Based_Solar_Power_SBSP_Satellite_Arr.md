# ## Automated Anomaly Detection and Predictive Maintenance in Space-Based Solar Power (SBSP) Satellite Arrays via Multi-Modal Data Fusion and HyperScore Evaluation

**Abstract:** This paper presents a novel framework for automated anomaly detection and predictive maintenance within Space-Based Solar Power (SBSP) satellite arrays, a rapidly developing area of renewable energy. Exploiting the geographically vast and environmentally harsh nature of SBSP operation, our system leverages a multi-modal data ingestion and normalization layer, combined with semantic decomposition and a robust evaluation pipeline using a HyperScore scoring system. The approach enables proactive identification of component degradation and potential failures, minimizing downtime and maximizing energy capture efficiency. This framework exemplifies an immediate application of advanced AI techniques to a critical emerging technology, with projected impact on the burgeoning space-based energy sector.

**1. Introduction**

Space-Based Solar Power (SBSP) promises a nearly limitless supply of clean energy, transmitted wirelessly from large satellite arrays in geosynchronous orbit to ground receiving stations. However, the extreme operational environmentâ€”high vacuum, intense solar radiation, micrometeoroid impacts, and thermal cyclingâ€”presents significant challenges to component reliability and overall system longevity. Traditional maintenance approaches are costly and require infrequent, specialized missions. This research addresses the need for an autonomous, predictive maintenance system capable of detecting anomalies and forecasting failures before they occur, minimizing operational downtime and maximizing energy harvest.  Our solution leverages advanced data fusion and machine learning techniques to analyze diverse datasets generated by the satellite array, providing a comprehensive assessment of system health and guiding preventative maintenance actions. The proposed system is commercializable within a 5-10 year timeframe, addressing a critical bottleneck in the widespread deployment of SBSP.
